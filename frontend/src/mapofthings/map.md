# Map of My Things

My journal is an artifact telling a story over time. Every day I try to snapshot everything. I try to report everything in my head, and everything in my life forever-and-all-at-once. It drags these threads out across days and doesn’t build, it tangles.

Working on too many things in my head at once. I can talk to different people about some of them, but I have no one to talk to about all of them, except Claude, and part of what’s in my head is that too many Claude conversations have spun off into too many open and uncompleted threads, that cannot be closed by Claude.

It just feels like it’s stuff that I’m trying to process entirely in my journal, I do all of my intellectual processing of all daily experiences; conversations, learnings, media, habits, project or social intentions, in my journal, crammed into 1.5 hours in the morning. Also my recent renewal of feedback practice I’ve been trying to hold onto many threads by dragging them forward day to day. It’s all tangled though. “Tangles in my brain box”, I call it, which never get sorted out and which I try “to carry with me”, even as I recognize again and again that pulling on threads doesn’t untangle them, and I cant’ carry it all with me. This map is a snapshot that stays current. I’ll delete the threads that expire, update the ones that change. And because I’m a coward, I’m putting it in version control to hold the developing artifact across time anyway.

2. Lack of human connection. Hoping to meet more people. Will said I need to find the activities that I would enjoy doing anyway, and meet people there. Struggle ongoing.

3. New strategy: acknowledge that I may have a low mood baseline. Instead try to focus on strengthening and experiencing the peaks.
Try to live in the moments of feeling shitty and don’t try to flee it. And I guess that’s it. Do things when I have a low mood ANYWAY and just accept that I’m going to feel low. Do things that still work even when I have low energy or enjoyment in things. That could mean walking, lying on the floor, doing chores.

Can I understand my brain chemistry better in order to take better control of my mood, experience, and agency?
Trying to think about neurochemicals like dopamine

More recently: “Am I neurodivergent”? Asked by a friend. I kind of hate it, and I don’t think that’s useful either. The useful version is not labels, but symptoms. What are my specific patterns that I need to either live with or leverage or work around strategically?

That’s a lack of material goals. My immaterial goals are: feel stronger feelings, be more engaged in the things that I am doing, find more things that I will find more engaging.
