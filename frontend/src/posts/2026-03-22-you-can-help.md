---
title: A Podcast concept called You Can Help
---

An idea I kicked around mostly in my head and then briefly with a friend, a fellow leftist looking for meaningful "project". Driven by a continued dissatisfaction with the options available for "doing meaningful work."

I like podcasts. I like thinking. I've been excited about the concept of "Anarchist Book Club" which I started summer of 2024 and it has sort of slouched along since, and I held onto it as basically the most important thing I was doing and I imagined the words "Anarchist Learning Collaborative" with thrill and feelings of grandeur. I emailed the address on the "Portland Free School" website and got no response. Another Portland Free School just does crafts like basketweaving.

Esther said: stop imagining the perfect group and just show up at the one that exists at DSA. Yeah ok well I finally did that, 4 times. I didn't like it at all. I didn't like the book selections, the people, the discussion structure.

I like intellectual conversations. Something like being on a podcast without a recording device.

"You Can Help": I struggle to find the ways to plug in to PROJECT and I think I'm not alone, a lot of people struggle to find ways to plug in. "The loneliness epidemic". So what if I could find ways to help? What if I could talk about those ideas, because I like talking about ideas. What if I could help people find ways that they can help?

That's the concept. I didn't actually have any episode ideas that I got especially excited about.
