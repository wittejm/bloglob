title: Narratives of I
---

This is an excerpt from morning journal pages, reflecting on my current reading of A World Appears, by Michael Pollan.

Theory vs practice: My theory of consciousness is PRACTICED by pursing concepts of brain chemistry. But THAT is still just theory. Which needs practice. Of some kind. This is just one development of one idea, at a time, in a disjoint manner, strung together by my embodied journal-- "I" am just one subjectivity in my brain, and there are many in there. Partially in communication with one another. Cognitively, a narrative of continuity of mind exists "in there", & it has an IMPRESSION of continuity because I am a BODY that persists in a continuous world, with a history, builds memory, & every time a DISPERSED AND PARTIAL cognitive process in my brain asks, "do I exist" is interprets its memories and feelings and reason to determine "yes" & nothing stops that from happening multiple times at once, or other cognitive processes to happen INSTEAD AND ALSO which are not actively expressing that narrative. So do I need a consistent narrative that is reinforced by a STRUCTURED NOTES OF MY IDEAS. or a different consistent narrative reinforced by a JOURNAL. Or a different narrative reinforced by FRIENDSHIPS, or a different narrative reinforced by PROJECTS, or a different narrative reinforced by GOALS. All of those narratives relate to a MIND-BOGGLINGLY COMPLEX cognitive concept which we call "I".
